Feijoada

Description

Feijoada is a stew of beans with beef and pork. The name feijoada is derived from feij√£o, 'bean' in Portuguese. It is widely prepared in the Portuguese-speaking world, with slight variations. The basic ingredients of feijoada are beans and fresh pork or beef. In Brazil, it is usually made with black beans.

ingredients

  • 1kg black beans
  • 70g pig ears
  • 70g pig's feet
  • 50g pork loin
  • 150g portuguese sausage
  • 100g dried meat
  • 70g pig's tail
  • 100g pork ribs
  • 100g paio sausage
  • 2 finely chopped onions
  • 3 bay leaves
  • Black pepper to taste
  • 40ml of pinga (alcohol)
  • 1 stem of spring onion
  • 6 garlic cloves
  • 1 or 2 oranges
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Soak the meats for 36 hours or more, change the water several times, if it is hot or summer, place ice on top or in cold layers.
  2. Cook step by step: hard meat, then soft meat.
  3. When soft, add the beans and remove the meat.
  4. Finally season the beans.
  5. Prepare accompaniments
